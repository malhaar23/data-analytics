The 60-second answer

  • You don't need coding. 90% of Data Analysis and SCM jobs at the entry level run on Excel, SQL, and dashboarding tools — not Python or engineering degrees.
  • Excel + SQL is your foundation. Master Excel + SQL before touching anything else. Every single analyst role on every job board lists them.
  • AI is your multiplier, not a shortcut. ChatGPT and Copilot can write SQL, explain data, and draft reports — but only if you understand enough to verify the output.
  • SCM is more analyst-heavy than people think. Demand planning, inventory analysis, and vendor data — it's all spreadsheets and dashboards.
  • Portfolio beats degree. 3 real projects + clean LinkedIn beats a premium certificate with nothing to show.

04How to actually use AI — the right way

AI doesn't replace you — it makes you 3x faster. But only if you know enough to guide it and verify what it gives you back.

TaskHow to use AI for itWhat to watch out for
Writing SQL queriesDescribe what you want in plain English. "Write a SQL query that shows total sales by region for last 3 months, ordered highest first." Copy, test, adjust.AI gets table/column names wrong — always check against your actual database schema.
Excel formulas"I have a column of dates in A and sales in B. Give me a formula to sum sales only for Q1." It will give you the formula and explain each part.Array formulas and complex nesting can have subtle errors — test with known values.
Explaining data to managementPaste your findings and say "Summarise this data insight in 3 bullet points for a non-technical audience." Great for presentation prep.AI may oversimplify. Add back your domain context and nuance before presenting.
Cleaning messy dataAsk "I have inconsistent city name spellings in Excel. What are 3 ways to standardise them?" or use Python + ChatGPT to write a cleaning script.Always back up raw data before running any cleaning script.
Learning faster"Explain SQL JOINs to me like I'm a commerce student with no coding background. Give me a real business example." Far better than most YouTube videos.Ask follow-up questions — don't stop at the first explanation.
Interview preparation"Give me 10 data analyst interview questions for a fresher at an e-commerce company in India. Then answer each one as a candidate would."Rehearse out loud — AI answers read better than they sound unrehearsed.
Writing your resumePaste your raw project description. "Rewrite this as a 2-line resume bullet using strong action verbs and quantified outcomes." Then edit to match your real numbers.AI inflates language. Verify every number and claim is something you can defend in an interview.

The rule for using AI at work

  • Use AI to draft, not to decide. AI gives you a starting point. You verify, adapt, and own the final output.
  • Learn the skill first, then use AI to speed it up. If you can't write a basic SQL query yourself, you won't catch when ChatGPT's SQL is wrong — and it often is.
  • Never paste confidential company data into ChatGPT. Use anonymised samples or synthetic data for work tasks.
  • The best prompt is a specific one. "Help me with SQL" gets a generic answer. "Write a SQL query to find top 5 SKUs by returns in the last 30 days, grouped by warehouse" gets something useful.

06Your 6-month roadmap

Don't try to learn everything before starting. This sequence gets you applying within 5–6 months — not 2 years.

Month 1
Excel mastery — go deep, not wide.
  • Learn Pivot Tables, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP, IF/IFS, SUMIF, and basic charts
  • Download a real sales dataset from Kaggle and build a dashboard from scratch
  • Goal: handle any spreadsheet task confidently without Googling
Month 2
SQL from zero.
  • Learn SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY, and JOIN — in that order
  • Practice 30 min daily on SQLZoo.net or Mode's free SQL Tutorial
  • End goal: answer "Which product category has the highest return rate?" in SQL
Month 3
Power BI + first real project.
  • Download Power BI Desktop (free) and connect it to a CSV dataset
  • Build a 3-page dashboard: overview, product breakdown, and trends
  • Publish to Power BI Service, add the live link to your LinkedIn — portfolio piece #1
Month 4
AI workflow + start applying.
  • Use ChatGPT daily for formula help, SQL practice, and mock interview prep
  • Start applying for internships — minimum 10 applications per week
  • Target e-commerce and FMCG companies first; they hire the most freshers
Month 5
Build a second project + SCM domain knowledge.
  • Pick a SCM-themed dataset — inventory, logistics, or procurement data
  • Build an Excel analysis or Power BI dashboard around a real business question
  • Study demand planning, OTIF, and inventory metrics — read 2–3 free McKinsey/BCG cases
Month 6
Interview prep + active applications.
  • Practise top 10 data analyst and SCM interview questions out loud — not just reading
  • Apply daily on LinkedIn, Naukri, Unstop, and company career pages
  • Don't wait to feel ready — readiness comes from real interviews, not more studying
After Job
Learn Python slowly, on the job.
  • Start Python in evenings — 20 min/day, pandas for data cleaning only
  • Cross the next salary band within 6–12 months with data + domain expertise
  • Add PL-300 or Google Data Analytics certification when your company sponsors it

07Myths vs reality

The internet makes this career sound either impossible (you need a CS degree) or easy (just do a 3-month bootcamp). Both are wrong.

Myths to ignore

  • "You need a CS/engineering degree" — BBA + Excel + SQL + portfolio gets you the same interviews. Domain knowledge from BBA is an advantage in business-facing roles.
  • "Do a paid bootcamp first" — Everything you need is free: Kaggle, SQLZoo, Microsoft Learn, YouTube. Bootcamps sell confidence, not skills.
  • "Learn Python before Excel" — Companies want Excel mastery. Python is the upgrade. Don't skip the foundation chasing the advanced tool.
  • "Get 5 certifications then apply" — Certifications signal intent. Projects signal ability. Hirers look at what you've built, not what courses you've taken.
  • "SCM is just logistics" — SCM today is heavily data-driven. Demand planners, procurement analysts, and inventory analysts are all analytical roles.
  • "AI will replace analysts" — AI replaces repetitive data pulling. It doesn't replace business judgment, stakeholder communication, or problem framing — all of which a BBA student is trained for.

What actually works

  • Learn Excel properly first — It's unsexy but it's the job. 80% of analyst tasks in Indian companies happen in Excel.
  • Build real projects on public data — One good project outweighs three certifications. Pick a business problem, solve it, publish it.
  • Use your BBA advantage — You understand P&L, marketing, finance, and business context. Tech people often don't. That's your edge — lead with it.
  • Apply before you feel ready — The job market gives you feedback no course can. Rejection is data. Interviews teach you what to improve.
  • LinkedIn is your biggest multiplier — A complete, active LinkedIn profile with your projects visible gets you inbound messages. Most candidates skip this.
  • Learn AI tools alongside skills — Use ChatGPT to practice SQL, explain concepts, prep for interviews. Treat it as a tutor, not a shortcut.

08How to land your first role

Getting the job is a separate skill from doing the job. Most people under-invest in the application and interview process.

WhatHow to do it right
Resume (1 page)
  • Lead with skills: Excel (Advanced), SQL (Intermediate), Power BI
  • Then projects, then education — keep to 1 page
  • Use ChatGPT to rewrite bullets: "Built a sales dashboard tracking ₹2Cr in revenue across 4 product lines"
LinkedIn profile
  • Headline: "Aspiring Data Analyst | Excel · SQL · Power BI | Open to Opportunities"
  • Add your Power BI dashboard as a featured project
  • Post once a week about what you're learning — recruiters actively search for this
Where to apply
  • LinkedIn Easy Apply, Naukri.com, Unstop (campus), company career pages
  • For SCM: also check Logistics Insider and supply chain LinkedIn groups
  • Apply on Day 1 of posting — response rates drop significantly after 48 hours
Interview prep
  • Know SQL basics cold enough to write a query live
  • Walk through a dashboard you built — practice this out loud
  • Prepare 1–2 business problems you've solved with data
Cold outreach
  • Message analysts at target companies on LinkedIn
  • "I'm a BBA student building data skills — can I get 15 minutes of your time?"
  • 1 in 5 will say yes — those conversations are worth more than any course
Internship first
  • If full-time isn't converting, take a 3-month internship — even low-paid
  • Real work experience moves you to a different resume tier entirely
  • Find them on Internshala, LinkedIn, and direct company career pages
